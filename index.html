About Me

I am a chemical (and later biomedical) engineer by training, but am also interested in and tapped into other engineering disciplines. Currently, I'm pursuing a PhD in ECE at University of Washington under the supervision of Dr. Kimberly Ingraham. My research interests include the design and control of exoskeletons and prostheses for rehabilitation and human augmentation. My past research experience includes a Master's in Biomedical Engineering from McGill University, supervised by Drs. David Juncker and Corinne F. Maurice, and research assistant work at Polytechnique Montréal.

Current Research

I am currently a PhD Candidate at the University of Washington, developing novel control schemes for wearable assistive devices with Dr. Kim Ingraham. My work focuses on the design and control of exoskeletons and prostheses for rehabilitation and human augmentation.

Past Projects

During my Master's at McGill University, I developed the Mini Colon Model (MiCoMo), a benchtop multi-bioreactor system to investigate the gut microbiome. As a Research Assistant at Polytechnique Montréal, I worked on biomechanics and exoskeletons, including modeling the dynamics of the human lower limb with exoskeletons and simulating control strategies.
